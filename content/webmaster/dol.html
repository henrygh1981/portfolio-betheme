    Department of Labor Website Redesign

    Completed by HenryG at May 10, 2021
    Categories
    • Web
    • Mobile
    My Role:

    UX/UI Designer

    Tools:

    Miro, Whimsical, Figma, GDrive, Zoom, StoryboardThat

    Prototype:

    Figma Prototype

    Presentation:

    Google Slides

    The Problem

    Overwhelming amount of information and inconsistent navigation system make it difficult to navigate smoothly around the website.

    Hypothesis

    Redesign of the website with navigation organized for a more modern and simplistic way to find necessary information efficiently can help visitors have more delightful experiences and find information they look for easily.

    User Persona - Meet Tiffany

    Tiffany, an hourly wage earner, is a hard worker who is having difficulty holding a good work life balance. She works longer hours and often brings work to home. Lately, she is feeling she may not be getting paid for all those extra hours she has been working for. She would like to learn more about the overtime pay policies and get a sense whether she is being paid for her complete overtime work. She would also like to find out whether there is a calculator where she enters her hours to find out what she should be getting paid.

    Heuristic Evaluation

    • Large pictures without any additional information
    • Less important information at the home page
    • “Topic” tab is not categorized. There is a hiden link on topic, which takes you to categorized menu.
    • Information overload and uncategorized
    • Navigation is not above the fold and also not consistent with other navigation bars/menus
    • Unstructured information and it’s relation to navigation is not clear

    Prototypes and Usability Testing

    With the Figma prototype users:

    • Navigates easily through pages for both web and mobile
    • Gathered information about overtime pay
